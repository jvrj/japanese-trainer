# Beginner Japanese — Research Synthesis

Compiled 2026-04-19 for Julius (adult beginner-intermediate, all-hiragana, goals: real conversation + Japan trip + sustained hobby). Sources span academic SLA literature, the Japanese-learning practitioner community (Refold, Tofugu, MaruMori, WaniKani, r/LearnJapanese, AJATT lineage), and the contrarian/skeptic camp.

---

## TL;DR

1. **Time:** 30–45 min/day, splittable into 2–3 sessions, beats any longer schedule you won't actually keep. Frequency >> duration. Attrition is the default; design around it.
2. **Input vs output:** Input-heavy (~70%) but **don't delay output past month 1–2** if conversation is your goal. Pure-input orthodoxy is the most-regretted methodology choice in the JP intermediate community.
3. **SRS:** Works, but tune it down from Anki defaults. **85% retention target, ~10 new cards/day, audio on every card, FSRS scheduling.** The biggest failure mode is the streak trap and recognition-without-production.
4. **Listening:** Audio matters from day one for the *phonological loop* (vocab acquisition bottleneck). Stick to **scaffolded comprehensible input** (Yuki / Teppei beginner) — passive anime-noise is mostly wasted hours at A0. Skip shadowing until intermediate.
5. **All-hiragana strategy:** Defensible for spoken goals; **brittle for the trip.** Real Japan is ~30% katakana in the menu/signage/product domain. Add katakana before flying — 2–3 weeks of work, prevents tourist illiteracy.

## 1. Daily time + split

### Verdict
**30–45 min/day, split if possible, every day.** The realistic floor for visible progress is 30 min; the ceiling for sustainable adult study is ~60–90 min. High-volume immersion plans (Refold/AJATT 4–6 hrs/day) reliably produce burnout before they produce fluency for working professionals.

### Where the three angles agree
- **Frequency beats duration.** Baddeley & Longman (1978) postal-worker typing study: 1 hr/day for 80 days outperformed 4 hr/day for 20 days at identical total hours. Distributed practice is the most replicated finding in cognitive psychology.
- **Attention degrades past 60–90 min.** Ericsson's deliberate-practice work supports 2×30 min over 1×60 min.
- **The "minimum viable day" is the most upvoted Reddit advice.** A 10-min fallback (just SRS reviews) on chaotic days beats skipping — because skipping compounds review debt and triggers the quit pattern.

### Where the angles split
- **Refold/AJATT:** more hours = faster, no real ceiling. Case studies cite ~500 hrs per 3 months. Skeptic camp documents this is exactly the pattern that produces month-3 burnout.
- **Pragmatists (Tofugu, MaruMori, Migaku):** 1 hr/day is the sweet spot; 30 min the sustainable floor.
- **Polyglot critics (Lampariello, Kaufmann):** consistency over 2+ years beats intensity over 6 months every time; intensity-first is survivor bias.

### Suggested split for a 45-min day
| Block | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|----------|
| SRS reviews | 15 min | Vocab + kana blitz (your PWA) |
| Listening | 15 min | Yuki / Nihongo con Teppei / Pimsleur |
| Grammar/output | 15 min | Genki, read-aloud, or 1×/week iTalki tutor |

## 2. Input vs. output balance at A0→A1

### Verdict
**Input-heavy (~70%) with low-stakes output starting in month 1–2.** Don't follow Refold/Matt-vs-Japan's "no output for 1000 hours" rule — for conversation/trip goals, that path produces a "silent intermediate" who understands but freezes when spoken to.

### Where the three angles agree
- **Some input is non-negotiable** before forced output. A0 learners with zero input produce fossilized translationese.
- **Read-aloud is universally endorsed** as A0-compatible output: low stakes, no bad-habit risk because you're copying native audio.
- **Refold itself has softened.** Their own 3B Output Troubleshooting page admits learners following the orthodoxy hit psychological walls.

### Where the angles split
- **Pure-input camp (Refold legacy):** delay output to avoid cementing errors.
- **Output-first camp (iTalki, Pimsleur, Glossika):** speak from week 1; speaking is a trainable skill, anxiety compounds.
- **Modern academic consensus** (post-Swain, post-Schmidt's noticing hypothesis): output forces syntactic processing and reveals gaps; input alone plateaus (proven by Canadian French-immersion failures).

### Most-cited regret
> "I waited too long to speak."

This dominates the JP intermediate regret threads. The opposite regret ("I spoke too early and locked in bad pronunciation") exists but is rarer and more fixable.

### Concrete recommendations for A0–A1
- **Month 1:** Read-aloud only. Echo Pimsleur or Teppei aloud. No solo speaking.
- **Month 2:** Add one 30-min iTalki community tutor session per week. Cheap, low-stakes, builds the speaking muscle.
- **Month 3+:** Maintain weekly tutor; start brief self-talk/journaling out loud.

### Key sources
- Swain (1985) — Output Hypothesis (built on Canadian immersion failures)
- Schmidt (1990) — Noticing Hypothesis

## 3. SRS done right

### Verdict
**SRS is the right tool for A0 Japanese vocab, but Anki defaults are wrong.** Drop retention target to **85%**, cap new cards at **~10–12/day**, put **audio on every card**, and use **FSRS** scheduling (default since Anki 23.10, Nov 2023). The biggest dangers are streak-worship, review-debt death spiral, and recognition-without-production.

### Where the three angles agree
- **Spacing effect and testing effect are bedrock** (Cepeda 2006, Karpicke & Roediger 2008). The basic mechanic works.
- **Wozniak's own SuperMemo research** suggests 85% retention is more time-efficient than the Anki 90% default.
- **FSRS solves "ease hell"** of the old SM-2 algorithm — the historical biggest beginner headache is now mostly obsolete.
- **Audio on cards is non-negotiable.** Baddeley's phonological loop is the long-term vocabulary bottleneck for adults; silent text-only cards under-train it.
- **Recognition cards alone are insufficient** — recognition ≠ production.

### Where the angles split
- **Card structure debate:** word cards (faster reviews, less context) vs. sentence cards (slower, contextualized). Consensus: word cards at A0, sentence cards once mining native content.
- **Production cards (EN→JP):** Tatsumoto/Animecards say skip; 80/20 Japanese ships them as toggleable. Skeptics say production is the missing piece if you only do recognition.
- **Anti-SRS polyglot critique** (Kaufmann, Lampariello — both 15+ languages, no Anki): "the streak metric replaces the goal." But — they learn European cognate-rich languages where SRS earns less. Japanese has ~zero cognates; SRS earns its keep at A0.

### Failure modes to actively guard against
1. **Streak-worship replacing the language goal** — you become excellent at the app, not at Japanese.
2. **Review debt death spiral** — miss 3 days, pile-up triggers quit. Cap daily review queue (~40 max).
3. **Zombie reviewing** — short gaps + large deck = answering by shape recognition, not meaning.
4. **Context-free vocabulary** — `食べる = "to eat"` is a different skill from `寿司を食べる`.
5. **The "8000-card learner who can't order coffee"** archetype — recognition-only path.

### Concrete settings
| Setting | Value | Source |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Retention target | 85% | Wozniak revisions; Bjork desirable difficulty |
| New cards/day | 10–12 | Migaku; Nation's 5–15 heuristic |
| Daily review cap | 40 | Anti-burnout guard; informal community |
| Algorithm | FSRS | Anki default since Nov 2023 |
| Audio | Mandatory | All three angles agree |
| Card type | Word + audio at A0 | Animecards / Tatsumoto |

### Key sources
- Cepeda, Pashler, Vul, Wixted & Rohrer (2006) — spacing meta-analysis, *Psychological Bulletin*

## 4. Listening + shadowing at hiragana stage

### Verdict
**Yes to scaffolded comprehensible input from day one. No to shadowing until intermediate. No to passive anime-as-noise.** The phonological loop (Baddeley) is the bottleneck for adult vocab acquisition — *some* JP audio exposure has to happen even at A0. But "input must be 95%+ comprehensible to drive acquisition" (Conti synthesis), which means almost nothing native is useful at A0 without scaffolding.

### Where the three angles agree
- **Audio-on-cards is the cheapest, highest-leverage addition** for any hiragana-stage tool.
- **Comprehensible Japanese (Yuki) and Nihongo con Teppei beginner** are the consensus first-listening resources.
- **Untrained shadowing fossilizes errors.** L1 phonology filters override the new sound until explicitly trained.

### Where the angles split
- **CI purists (Yuki / Dreaming Spanish-inspired):** visual-supported CI is the only real beginner listening.
- **AJATT camp:** just have JP playing constantly; comprehension grows subconsciously. Skeptics: this is wishful thinking — input below 95% comprehensibility is functionally useless (Conti).
- **Shadowing advocates** (Step Up Japanese): works at any level if material is level-appropriate. **Skeptics & SLA review** (Tandfonline 2025): "results inconclusive" for segmental pronunciation; A0 self-studiers almost never scaffold properly.

### Concrete recommendations
- **Day 1 onwards:** [Comprehensible Japanese (Yuki) — Complete Beginner playlist](https://cijapanese.com/) + [Nihongo con Teppei for Beginners](https://open.spotify.com/show/4W4jYoKRmjlURKO1fIfcOK) (~4 min episodes).
- **Add to commute / chores:** Pimsleur Japanese Level 1 — audio-only, forces active recall, hiragana-compatible (no reading required), trip-aligned.
- **Skip for now:** anime, J-dramas, movies. Keep for warm motivation, not study credit.
- **Defer shadowing:** wait until you can comfortably handle Teppei beginner episodes before any shadowing attempts.

### Key sources
- Baddeley, Gathercole & Papagno (1998) — phonological loop as language-learning device
- Hamada (2016), *Language Teaching Research* — shadowing meta-review
- [Conti — Why input must be 95–98% comprehensible](https://gianfrancoconti.com/2025/02/27/why-the-input-we-give-our-learners-must-be-95-98-comprehensible-in-order-to-enhance-language-acquisition-the-theory-and-the-research-evidence/)
- [Tandfonline 2025 — shadowing systematic review](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/29984475.2025.2546827)
- [Frontiers 2025 — neuro-ecological critique of Krashen](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1636777/full)

## 5. The all-hiragana strategy — honest critique

This was the bonus question you didn't get to vote on, but it shapes everything else. So here's the unvarnished take.

### Verdict
**Defensible for spoken-conversation goals. Brittle for the Japan trip.** The PWA can be hiragana-only forever and that's fine for vocab/conversation drilling — but you cannot read modern Japan in hiragana. **Add katakana before the trip; defer kanji indefinitely if you want.**

### The case against staying hiragana-only
- **Katakana is ~30% of the script in the tourist domain** (menus, product labels, signage, hotel forms, station names for foreign places). Loanwords are *natively* katakana: コーヒー, ビール, トイレ, ホテル, タクシー.
- **Hiragana-only adult texts don't exist.** Outside first-grade primers, no real Japanese is written this way. Native readers find pure-hiragana actively confusing.
- **Homophone disambiguation is structural.** かえる = 帰る/蛙/変える. ここではきものをぬいでください ("take off shoes" vs. "take off kimono"). Kanji isn't ornament — it's how the language disambiguates.
- **Katakana costs 2–3 weeks** of casual study. The "delay" saves days and costs months of downstream friction.

### The case for spoken/PWA hiragana-only
- **Phonology is fully captured by kana.** A toddler speaks fluently with zero kanji. If your goal is *spoken* recall, the script is interface cosmetics.
- **The PWA's "vocab blitz" pattern is about meaning↔sound mapping**, which works in hiragana.

### Practical resolution
| Use | Script |
|-----|--------|
| Isshin PWA vocab drilling | Hiragana ✓ (current) |
| Spoken conversation goal | Hiragana ✓ (script-irrelevant for speech) |
| Pre-trip reading prep | **Add katakana** (2–3 weeks before flight) |
| Reading manga / signs / menus | Katakana required + 200–500 common kanji |
| Long-term (post-trip) | Optional: 200 most common kanji via radicals |

## Three-month progression

- **Month 1:** Anchor the daily habit. Isshin + Yuki/Teppei. Read aloud. **No solo speaking yet.**
- **Month 2:** Add iTalki weekly. Add Pimsleur L1 to commute. **Add katakana** (2–3 weeks of work, fold into Isshin if you build it; Tofugu's mnemonic guide otherwise).
- **Month 3:** Maintain. Reassess: are you understanding more Teppei? Can you handle a 30-min iTalki without freezing? If yes, consider Genki II or sentence-mining. If not, don't push — consistency wins.

## What to actively avoid

- **No anime/J-drama as study time** at A0. Keep them as motivation snacks, not learning.
- **No 2-hour weekend cram sessions.** They produce nothing; consistency does.
- **No pure-input dogma.** Speak before month 3.
- **No new SRS app.** Isshin is fine. Don't shop for tools when the tool you have works.
- **No Refold roadmap.** Wrong shape for your goals.

# Isshin PWA — Implications

Ranked by leverage. Per the ship-and-see memory, **none of this is a roadmap** — it's a menu of evidence-backed candidates. Pick what you actually feel friction on.

## Tier 1 — directly evidence-backed, low-effort

1. **Audio on every card.** Phonological-loop research is unanimous: silent JP cards under-train the bottleneck for adult vocab. TTS counts. This is the single highest-leverage addition if any cards lack audio.
2. **Drop retention target from 90% → 85%** if Isshin uses Anki-style ease scheduling. Wozniak's own revisions support it; more retrieval per minute, less time spent.
3. **Cap daily review queue (~40).** Prevents the review-debt death spiral that triggers quitting after a missed week.
4. **New-card cap at ~10–12/day.** Already may be handled implicitly; if not, set a hard cap.

## Tier 2 — addresses real failure modes, requires design

5. **Production mode that's actually production.** The "Mixed Form Blitz" mislabel issue from the previous DA still stands — currently it's JP→EN (recognition) labeled as recall. Either flip it to EN→JP or audio-only ("hear it, type it") to combat the silent-intermediate trap.
6. **Katakana mode.** The all-hiragana strategy has a real cliff at the trip. Adding katakana to Isshin (mirror of Kana Blitz) is in scope and pays off concretely.
7. **De-emphasize the streak counter.** The Lampariello/My Senpai streak-trap critique is real. Replace or downgrade with a milestone tally tied to the goal ("words used in real speech this week" — manual log).

## Tier 3 — interesting, probably defer

8. **Sentence/context on card backs.** Addresses Kaufmann's context-free critique. Costs UX complexity; benefits are real but not urgent at A0.
9. **"Show real-world form" toggle** — passively expose the user to katakana/kanji forms even when drilling in hiragana. Cheap, future-proofs without disrupting current flow.
10. **Soft pause / grace mechanic** for missed days to prevent the death spiral. Less urgent than the cap (#3).

## Tier 4 — explicitly out of scope

- LLM-tutor features, conversational practice, grammar lessons. Per validated scope: Isshin is a word-recall tool, not a tutor. Speaking goes to iTalki, listening to Yuki/Teppei, grammar to Genki.

# Honest gaps in this research

- No peer-reviewed study directly addresses "adult beginner Japanese learner using a custom SRS PWA." Everything is extrapolated from adjacent literature (general memory research, EFL studies, polyglot-community heuristics).
- The 85% vs 90% retention number is best-guess from Wozniak's own writings — no published RCT on adult L2 learners directly compares.
- "Optimal" daily minutes for adult L2 has no SLA evidence base; the 30–45 min recommendation is a synthesis of cognitive-psych research, community polling, and attrition data.
- The all-hiragana critique is mostly community + practical reasoning, not academic. There are no peer-reviewed studies of "delayed katakana" outcomes specifically.
